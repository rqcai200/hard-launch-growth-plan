# Your Vibe Coding Growth Plan
**For Product Managers**

---

## Why this skill matters right now

AI prototyping tools like Cursor, Lovable, and Claude Code let you build working software through natural language. For PMs, this means you can go from product idea to clickable prototype in an afternoon without waiting on engineering. Companies including Shopify have added AI prototyping to their PM interview loops. If you're not building yet, you're falling behind peers who are.

> "So far, designers always had Dribbble, engineers always had GitHub, and they could show their portfolio to other people. For PMs, for the first time, you can actually show what you can build -- your product sense and everything -- by creating a portfolio of products." — Deepak Singh, ex-Amazon PM

## Level 1: Foundation

**Goal:** Build your first working prototype this week. No coding experience needed.

What you'll learn:
- How to turn a product requirement into a prompt that produces a working UI
- Which tool to use for what (Lovable, v0, Cursor, Bolt, Claude Code)
- When to vibe code vs. when to spec it out and hand off to eng

> "Previously, you always had to ask someone else, can we build this? And now you can just talk with AI to do this." — Harold Dijkstra

**Free Lightning Lessons:**

**[AI Prototyping for Product Managers in 2025](https://maven.com/p/30546f)** — Colin Matthews
Build your first functional prototype in 10 minutes. Covers matching your product's existing design system so the prototype feels real to your team.

> "The main difference is not the prompt. I want to make this super, super clear. It is the starting point that we have. The fact that we've already built the components and we are reusing them -- that gives us higher fidelity prototypes without super complex prompts or fighting with the tool." — Colin Matthews

**[How to Start Prototyping with AI](https://maven.com/p/27b600)** — Harold Dijkstra
How to use v0 and ready-made components from 21st.dev to spin up prototypes for idea validation or stakeholder buy-in.

**[Create MVPs and Prototypes with AI Coding Tools](https://maven.com/p/5d4c64)** — Corentin Cremet
The full AI product lifecycle from ideation through post-launch maintenance. Good for understanding the complete picture before you dive in.

**[Vibe Coding 101: Kickstart 2026](https://maven.com/p/3ee8d0)** — Harold Dijkstra
The fundamentals: what vibe coding is, why it works, and building your first AI app without writing code.

## Level 2: Application

**Goal:** Use AI prototyping to make better product decisions and work more effectively with your team.

What you'll learn:
- How to prototype to validate hypotheses before committing eng time
- How to organize prototyping workflows across your team
- How to handle PM interviews that include vibe coding

> "Picking the right things to work on is perhaps more important than ever, because now the bottleneck isn't writing code -- it's actually figuring out what the product should look and feel like. If it's cheap to make this thing, then thinking deliberately returns back to having more value." — Aman Khan

**Free Lightning Lessons:**

**[How to Vibe-Code in PM Interviews](https://maven.com/p/d0a4bf)** — Deepak Singh
AI prototyping is showing up in PM interviews, especially for AI PM roles. Covers the three skills being tested: AI prototyping, technical system design, and root-cause analysis.

> "If you are not doing rapid prototyping in your pursuit to product-market fit, one, you're losing a lot of time because you are going to software developers, they are coding it, and then you are reiterating. And second, somebody else is building very fast using these tools, which means they will have the first mover advantage to PMF." — Deepak Singh

**[Adopt AI Prototypes With Your Team](https://maven.com/p/ad13e2)** — Colin Matthews
Getting your team to try v0 or Bolt is step one. This covers reusable baselines, organizing prototypes, and tracking which ones are active vs. abandoned.

> "One of the very early students in my cohort works at Todoist, and they adopted AI prototyping across the team. After the course, they had enrolled their whole team, and then about four or five months later, PMs were able to ship really small features in production." — Colin Matthews

**[How Super ICs Use No-Code x AI](https://maven.com/p/c71d5a)** — Harold Dijkstra
Replace docs and spreadsheets with usable internal tools by combining no-code building blocks with AI. Practical for PMs who want to solve real problems inside their team.

**Courses:**

**[AI Prototyping for Product Managers](https://maven.com/tech-for-product/ai-prototyping-for-product-managers)** — Colin Matthews
Multi-cohort, hands-on course. Takes you from first prototype through integrating AI prototyping into your team's product development workflow.

**[Claude Code for Product Managers](https://maven.com/aman-khan/claude-code-for-product-managers)** — Aman Khan
Goes beyond visual prototyping tools. How to use Claude Code as a PM for research, analysis, and building features.

> "You're using Cursor as a thinking partner on a codebase to find product opportunities -- kicking it off to understand what is this codebase, try the product out itself, open it, test it, and see, here's pitfalls, here's opportunities to improve. And you haven't written a single line of code." — Aman Khan

**[Prototype to Production: The AI PM Playbook](https://maven.com/aman-khan/thriving-as-an-ai-pm)** — Aman Khan
Covers the transition from "I have a working prototype" to "we shipped an AI product," which is where most PMs get stuck.

## Level 3: Leverage

**Goal:** Go from prototyping to shipping production apps, and understand the technical landscape well enough to lead AI product conversations.

What you'll learn:
- What breaks when you move from demo to production, and how to handle it
- How to read and understand the code AI generates for you
- How agents and agentic architectures work at a practical level

> "Even though it's become easier than ever to build software, this has created a new problem, which I'll call Vibe Coding Hell -- basically this situation where you're generating code faster than you can understand it." — Shaw Talebi

**Free Lightning Lessons:**

**[Turn AI Demos Into Real Products in 30 Min](https://maven.com/p/102f4c)** — Aki Wijesundara
The five things that break going from demo to production: hallucinations, latency spikes, cost explosions, eval gaps, and silent failures. Covers what changes when real users show up.

**[End-to-End Production Vibe Coding Process](https://maven.com/p/ab585d)** — Colin Matthews
An 8-step workflow for validating, building, and launching vibe coded SaaS apps. Based on a process that led to a production app acquisition in under 90 days.

> "The main difference in terms of getting stuff to feel like a real app versus an AI-generated app is not so much who's doing the coding -- it's more so how much have you thought about what you want, and are you being specific and intentional with what you're building." — Colin Matthews

**[How to Build a Python Tutor with Claude Code](https://maven.com/p/f4447d)** — Shaw Talebi
Addresses "vibe coding hell" head-on. Learn system prompts, output styles, and how to control Claude's behavior so you understand what you're building.

> "Even though you can spin up entire applications using English via these coding agents, it's still helpful to understand a little bit of how programming works, understanding the language that you're implementing the application in, and just be able to understand how coding works." — Shaw Talebi

**[From Coder to Architect: Coordinate a Team of AI Agents](https://maven.com/p/3481f1)** — Carmelo Iaria
How to define agent personas, create Cursor rules, and set up coordination protocols across multiple AI agents. For PMs who want to understand what agent-first development looks like.

> "Building an agent for yourself could be extremely hard depending on where you are. But the second best thing you can do is to build workflows, because you'll create a mental model on how to do structured thinking when it comes to building agents. A person who can build very good workflows can actually build good agents also, because the fundamentals are the same." — Deepak Singh

**Courses:**

**[Vibe Code Production-Ready Side Projects](https://maven.com/tech-for-product/vibe-code-your-next-side-project)** — Colin Matthews
CI/CD pipelines, unit tests, integration tests, and security, taught for people who aren't engineers.

**[AI Builders Bootcamp](https://maven.com/shaw-talebi/ai-builders-bootcamp)** — Shaw Talebi
Six sessions covering coding with AI, prompt engineering, RAG, agents, fine-tuning, and shipping. For PMs who want to understand the full technical stack.

**[OpenClaw & Claude Code Certification for AI-Native PMs](https://maven.com/marily-nika/ai-agent-certification)** — Marily Nika
Certification program for PMs building AI-native products. Pairs a credential with hands-on agent building.
